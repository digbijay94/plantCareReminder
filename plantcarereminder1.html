Spider Plant:



Watering:

Water your spider plant when the top 50-75% of the soil is dry. Overwatering can lead to root rot, while underwatering can cause the leaf tips to turn brown. Use distilled or rainwater if possible, as spider plants are sensitive to fluoride and chlorine in tap water23.

Humidity:

Spider plants prefer moderate humidity. If the air is too dry, you might notice brown leaf tips. Misting the plant regularly can help maintain the right humidity levels13.

Temprature:

Keep your spider plant in a room with temperatures between 60-80°F (15-27°C). They can tolerate cooler temperatures but should be protected from frost13.

Soil:

Use a well-draining potting mix. Spider plants prefer slightly moist soil but do not like to sit in water24.

Fertilizing:

Feed your spider plant with a balanced, all-purpose fertilizer once a month d uring the growing season (spring and summer). Reduce feeding in the fall and winter15.

Pest controll:

Keep an eye out for common pests like spider mites and aphids. If you notice any, treat the plant with insecticidal soap or neem oil2. These tips should help you maintain a healthy and vibrant spider plant.
